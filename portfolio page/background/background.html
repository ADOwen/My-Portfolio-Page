About me

I'm a first generation American. My parents are from Ukraine and came here in the 70's looking for opportunity. I grew up in Brooklyn with my two elder sisters. We lived the simple city life. My parents would take us upstate for the summers to bungalow colonies. My parents friends that immigrated here from the same region, would go there with all their kids as well. So it was like a small community and everybody knew everybody. For spring break we would all drive down and stay in the same hotels. I gained a lot of strong friendships through this part of my life, which I'm thankful to my parents for.


I started working very early in my life, I always wanted to be independent when I was young. When I was too young to work, I would break and tie up boxes for this restaruant in exchange for burgers or ice cream. My sister still teases me until this day. I worked a lot of odd jobs until I started working as a pharmacy technician. That's when I started trying to discover what I wanted to do in life. I opened a retail food business, studied abroad, came back, met my wife and then decided to study the sciences. I got an associates in biology and at the time the plan was to get a steady high paying job. I decided I would be a dentist just for that reason. Except I didn't like anything about it.


In April of 2017, I lost my middle sister, she passed away unexpectedly at the age of 33. As you can imagine this was a big hit for the family. It changed my perspective on life and I decided that I didn't want to waste it doing something I didn't like. So I decided to throw myself into cooking even though it was logistically risky but it is what I'm passionate about. My wife and I moved to houston around the time, but we got hit hard there with hurrican harvey. We eventually moved back to NYC and things were going well until Covid hit. It hit the cooking industry hard but it was just another obstacle to overcome. My cooking days are not over but here I am ready or a new chapter in my story and I'm excited to get started.
